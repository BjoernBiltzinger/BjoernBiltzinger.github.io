My name is Björn Biltzinger and I am currently a PhD student in experimental astrophysics at the Max-Planck Institute for extraterrestrial physics in Garching (Munich). I am especially interested in statistical data analysis and modern software development.

I was always very interested in mathematics and natural sciences, which was the reason to start my physics studies at the Technical University Munich in 2013. During my studies I was getting more and more interested in experimental physics and data analysis, especially in the question if we can trust all experimental results or if there are problems with current data analysis techniques that can lead to wrong claims. This was also fueled by my master thesis, which I wrote at the Max-Planck Institute for extraterrestrial physics in Garching. The group in which I was working there was focused mainly on data analysis and developed many new sophisticated analysis techniques, from which I learned a lot about data analysis and statistics but also software development (mostly in Python). After I finished my master studies in 2019, I was offered a PhD position in the same group, which I was happy to accept, to continue my work with my awesome co-workers in this group and to deepen my data analysis and software development skills. During this time I lead several scientific [publications]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link _pages/publications.md %}), but also my focus slowly shifted from the physics behind it more to the software developing and statistics part of the work. Which is one of the reasons why I will leave academia after my PhD studies.

So when I finish my PhD thesis (mid 2023) I will be looking for jobs in the software development/data science industry.
