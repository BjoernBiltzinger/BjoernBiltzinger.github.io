My name is Björn Biltzinger and I am a Senior Software and Data Engineer at hema.to (https://www.hema.to/). Before that I did my PhD in experimental astrophysics at the Max-Planck Institute for extraterrestrial physics in Garching (Munich). I am especially interested in statistical data analysis and modern software development.

I was always very interested in mathematics and natural sciences, which was the reason to start my physics studies at the Technical University Munich in 2013. During my studies I was getting more and more interested in experimental physics and data analysis, especially in the question if we can trust all experimental results or if there are problems with current data analysis techniques that can lead to wrong claims. This was also fueled by my master thesis, which I wrote at the Max-Planck Institute for extraterrestrial physics in Garching. The group in which I was working there was focused mainly on data analysis and modeling and developed many new sophisticated analysis techniques, from which I learned a lot about data analysis and statistics but also software development (mostly in Python and Stan). After I finished my master studies in 2019, I was offered a PhD position in the same group, which I was happy to accept, to continue my work with my awesome co-workers in this group and to deepen my data analysis and software development skills. During this time I lead several scientific [publications]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link _pages/publications.md %}), but also my focus slowly shifted from the physics behind it more to the software developing and statistics part of the work. Which was one of the reasons to leave academia after a short Postdoc position at the Max-Planck Institute for extraterrestrial physics.

Since July 2023 I am now a Senior Software and Data Engineer at hema.to, where I am part of a great team that works towards improving flow cytometry analysis for Leukemia diagnostics with machine learning. Here I work mainly on developing advanced MLOps pipelines but also develop new machnine learning models myself.
